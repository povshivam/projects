Arrangement of the Periodic Table

  • Periods - rows on the periodic table
  • Groups - columns on the periodic table
  • Metals - generally shiny elements that are a good conductor of electricity and thermal energy
  • Alkali Metals - elements in group 1; react quickly with other elements and have a silvery appearance when pure; have the lowest densities of all metals and occur only in compounds
  • Alkaline Earth Metals - elements in group 2; react almost as quick as alkali metals, as well as being soft and silvery; they have low density but higher than alkali metals
  • Transition Elements - elements in groups 3-12; all are metals with higher densities, melting points, and strength than groups 1 & 2; some exist naturally in pure form and are commonly used as building materials
  • Lanthanide Series - first row of transition elements underneath the periodic table; some are used to make strong magnets
  • Actinide Series - first row of transition elements underneath the periodic table; plutonium is used a fuel in some nuclear reactors
  • Metalloids - elements with chemical and physical of both nonmetals and metals
  • Nonmetals - elements without the properties of metals
  • Halogens - elements in group 17; react with a metal to form a salt and only occur as compounds naturally
  • Noble Gases - elements in group 18; only react with other elements in special cases and were discovered after Dimitri Mendeleev constructed his table
